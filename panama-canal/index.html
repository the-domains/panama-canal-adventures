Panama Canal

Some said it couldn't be done. But in 1870, scientists, engineers, explorers and, of course, politicians set out to prove it could. They plunged into the single largest and costliest engineering effort since the Egyptians constructed the pyramids.

So began the Panama Canal. During the next 40 years, an amazing human drama would play out that propelled some people and nations into the spotlight and ruined the reputations of others. But the story began long before the first shovel of dirt was dug.
